So why are these colours the most common?

  • Red (77%): red has historically symbolised strength and power across various cultures, as well as blood and sacrifice, while the colour itself is very visible to the human eye.
  • White (69%): white represents peace, purity and unity, and contrasts well with strong colours such as red.
  • Blue (51%): blue symbolises freedom and strength and can represent both the sea and sky.
  • Yellow/gold (43%): yellow or gold can symbolise the sun, wealth and energy.
  • Green (41%): green can symbolise agriculture and fertility, but is also a central colour to Islam.
  • Black (21%): black represents power and strength, and like white contrasts well with strong colours like red.
